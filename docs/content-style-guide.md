# Rooftop Twenty Two — Content Writing Style Brief

*A voice guide for writing and editing site copy. Referenced from `CLAUDE.md` so any copy Claude Code writes or edits sounds like us — not like an agency, and not like AI.*

*Built from how we actually talk, not from how websites are "supposed" to read.*

---

## The whole thing in one line

**We're across-the-table talkers.** Write every line the way we'd say it sitting across a table from a client: plain, warm, honest, straight to the point. If you wouldn't say it out loud to someone's face, don't write it.

That's the test for the whole site, including emails. Read it back as if you're saying it to a client over a coffee. If it sounds like a brochure, a LinkedIn post, or a robot, rewrite it until it sounds like a person.

---

## How we actually sound

These are pulled from how we really speak. Match this, not "professional web copy".

1. **Short sentences. Full stops do the work.** We talk in quick, punchy lines, not long flowing ones. "It's up to you to get it done." "No one's thinking about the client here." Let a point land on its own, then move on.
2. **Honest to a fault, including about ourselves.** We name the problem out loud. We'd rather under-promise than oversell. We don't dress a small thing up as a big thing.
3. **Relationship-first warmth.** We're a relationship business and it shows. Pick up the phone. A friendly voice. We put our arms around clients. We talk to people like we actually like them, because we do.
4. **Allergic to faff and to making people wait.** We hate a client asking for a simple thing and waiting a week for it. "Where's my button?" energy. Speed and care, no run-around.
5. **Confident, but modest.** Award-winning work, said plainly. We can call ourselves Dublin's best-kept secret without puffing our chest out. Show it, don't shout it.

---

## What we believe (let this colour the tone)

- **The client comes first, always.** Every line should pass "does this help the person reading it?" If a sentence is really about us showing off, cut it.
- **Results that matter, not vanity.** We talk about the stuff that moves a business: leads, pipeline, sales, revenue. Not awards we won or hours we billed.
- **Value, not hours.** We sell the outcome. We never talk to clients in terms of "the hours you have left". That's an internal thing, never copy.
- **On the pulse, but grounded.** We're ahead on tech and AI, but it's always in service of the client and the work, never tech for the sake of it.
- **Proactive.** We're the agency that rings about Christmas in May. Copy should feel like people who are already thinking three steps ahead for you.

## Don't sound like AI

This matters to us specifically. Clients can spot AI copy and it cheapens the work. The tells we watch for:

- **Em dashes everywhere.** This is the number-one giveaway. Don't string clauses together with em dashes. Break them into separate sentences with full stops. That's also just how we actually talk. One em dash on a page is plenty; zero is fine.
- **No emojis.** Not in body copy, not in headings, not in CTAs. (Hard rule unless a client brief explicitly asks.)
- **No "AI gloss".** The over-smooth, over-balanced, every-sentence-the-same-length rhythm. Vary it. Use a fragment. Ask a question. Sound like a human who's a bit in a hurry.
- **Never take the first draft as gospel.** Generic, padded, says-nothing copy is worse than a short honest line. If a sentence could sit on any agency's site, it's not ours yet.
- **Cut the AI vocabulary:** "in today's fast-paced digital landscape", "we're passionate about", "elevate", "unlock", "leverage", "seamless", "robust", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "delve", "tailored solutions". If you'd never say it across the table, bin it.

## Mechanics & house style

**Spelling: Irish/British English, always.** optimis**e**, recognis**e**, programm**e**, behaviour, centre, organis**e**, colour. Never American (-ize, -or, program, color). Lowercase "euro", "€" for figures.

**Fix comma splices with a full stop, not an em dash.** Our old instinct was to join a list to its payoff with a comma. Don't. Use a full stop and a short follow-up sentence. It reads truer to how we speak and dodges the AI tell.
- ✗ "Strategy, creative, and performance under one roof, the growth team that works like it's part of yours."
- ✓ "Strategy, creative, and performance, all under one roof. A growth team that works like it's part of yours."

**Headlines.** Short and declarative. A fragment is fine. Keep any emphasis to one phrase. Sentence case, not Title Case, including buttons.

**Conversational connectors are fine in moderation.** "Look", "So", "Here's the thing", "in fairness" read as us, but a little goes a long way in writing. Don't open every paragraph with one.

**Rhetorical questions earn their place.** We use them to make a point ("Where's my button?"). Good for hooks and sub-heads. Don't overdo it.

**Numbers and specifics beat adjectives.** "Five to ten good enquiries a month" beats "significant growth". Digits for stats. En dash for ranges (3–6, not 3-6). If there's no real number, make the point plainly rather than inflating it.

**CTAs sound like a person asking.** "Tell us what you're working on." "Pick up the phone." "Let's have a chat." Avoid "Submit", "Learn more", "Get started".

**Contractions on.** we're, you'll, don't, it's. We're not writing a contract.

## Vocabulary

**Words and phrases that are ours (use naturally, don't crowbar):**
*stuck in the mud, bread and butter, pick up the phone, a friendly voice, put our arms around them, under one roof, best version of ourselves, skin in the game, Dublin's best-kept secret, here nor there, no run-around.*

> Rotation rule: "under one roof" is a strong, true line for us, but it's everywhere on the current site. Use it once per page, where it lands hardest. Say the same idea fresh elsewhere.

**Words we like:** straight, honest, joined-up, in-house, proper, sharp, fast, proactive, results, leads, pipeline, sound (as in "they're sound").

**Bin these:** "Don't just take our word for it", "passionate", "elevate/unlock/leverage", "seamless", "synergy", "value-add", "take it to the next level", anything with an exclamation mark unless it's genuinely warm.

## Before → after (in our voice)

| Off-voice (too AI / too agency) | On-voice (across the table) |
|---|---|
| "We leverage cutting-edge digital solutions to elevate your brand and maximise ROI." | "We build the things that bring you customers. Then we own the results." |
| "Our passionate team delivers best-in-class results across all channels." | "One in-house team. We treat your growth like our own, and we measure it the way you do." |
| "Contact us today to learn more about our comprehensive service offerings." | "Tell us what you're working on. We'll come back with a straight answer and a time to talk." |
| "Our streamlined web process ensures seamless delivery." | "You shouldn't have to wait a week for a button. We don't make you wait." |
| "We achieved significant growth across key performance metrics." | "Two enquiries a month became eight. Google Ads conversions up 10%." |

## Page patterns (quick guide)

- **Hero:** one plain promise, one line of substance, two CTAs (do the thing / see the work). No paragraph. No em dashes.
- **Service page:** what you get → how we actually do it (plain English, honest) → what's included (concrete) → the steps → proof → an FAQ that answers what clients are actually nervous about (how long, how much, switching from another agency). The FAQ is where across-the-table honesty matters most.
- **Case study:** Overview → Challenge → Solution → **Results with real numbers**. Lead with the strongest number we can stand over. If a number's small or soft, make the point in words instead of publishing a weak figure as a headline.
- **Closing CTA / contact:** warm, low-pressure, human. "Tell us what you're scaling, launching, or rebuilding. We'll come back with a thoughtful reply and a time to talk it through." This is already right, keep it.

## The 10-second check (run on every line)

1. **Would I say this across the table to a client?** If not, rewrite.
2. **Is it short and plain, or am I padding it?** Cut to the point.
3. **Have I em-dashed clauses together?** Break them into full stops.
4. **Any emoji, gloss, or AI vocabulary?** Strip it.
5. **Irish/British spelling?** Check -ise, programme, colour, behaviour.
6. **Vague claim where a real one could go?** Add the number, or say it plainly.
7. **Repeating "under one roof" or a signature line that's already nearby?** Vary it.
